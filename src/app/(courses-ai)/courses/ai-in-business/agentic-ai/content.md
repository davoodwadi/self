# Topic
Agentic AI and Vibe Coding

## Slide 1: Introduction to the New Paradigm
- The software landscape between 2025 and 2026 has undergone a radical shift, moving away from manual syntax writing.
- Two concepts define this era: Agentic AI (the technological engine) and Vibe Coding (the human cultural response).
- The barrier to creating software has plummeted, sparking industry debates about technical debt and the future of human engineers.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** How has your own interaction with technology and software creation changed since the introduction of advanced AI?

## Slide 2: Defining Agentic AI
- Agentic AI refers to artificial intelligence systems designed to act autonomously, pursue defined goals, and execute decisions with minimal human intervention.
- The industry has fully transitioned from the 2024 "AI Copilots" (which required step-by-step guidance) to the 2026 "Autonomous Agents."
- Agents are now stepping outside of the IDE to negotiate, research, and execute tasks on behalf of users.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** Think of a complex, multi-step task in your daily job. How would an autonomous "agent" handle it differently than an assistant "copilot"?

## Slide 3: The Microservices Moment for AI
- Instead of relying on a single, monolithic Large Language Model (LLM), development now relies on multi-agent orchestration.
- An orchestrator agent coordinates specialized sub-agents working in parallel (e.g., a data analyst agent, a coding agent, and a QA agent).
- This mirrors the software architecture shift from monolithic applications to microservices.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** If you had to build a team of specialized AI agents for your current project, what specific roles and responsibilities would you assign to them?

## Slide 4: Extended Autonomous Execution
- In early 2025, AI handled one-shot tasks that took minutes (e.g., "fix this bug").
- By 2026, agents are capable of working continuously for days, navigating complex legacy codebases, and building entire applications autonomously.
- Humans now provide strategic oversight at key decision checkpoints rather than micro-managing the code.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** What are the potential risks of giving AI continuous, autonomous execution capabilities without continuous human monitoring?

## Slide 5: Governance-First Design
- Because agents now take autonomous actions, security and governance cannot be an afterthought.
- "Governance-first design" is central to enterprise AI, establishing strict permission boundaries and decision logs.
- Systems are built with human-in-the-loop approval checkpoints to prevent rogue or expensive automated actions.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** In an automated software development lifecycle, where are the most critical points to place a mandatory "human-in-the-loop" checkpoint?

## Slide 6: The "Vibe Coding" Phenomenon
- Coined in February 2025 by Andrej Karpathy, Vibe Coding describes a chatbot-based approach to software development.
- Users express their intentions in high-level plain speech (the "vibes"), and the AI handles all the underlying syntax and execution.
- Its cultural impact was so massive that "vibe coding" became the Collins English Dictionary Word of the Year for 2025.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** Do you feel that writing software based on "vibes" makes technology more accessible, or does it inherently reduce the quality of the final product?

## Slide 7: The "Code First, Refine Later" Philosophy
- Vibe coding prioritizes momentum, rapid experimentation, and immediate results over structural planning.
- Creators use natural language prompts in tools like Cursor or Claude Code, accepting the generated source code without reviewing the internal logic.
- Bugs are fixed via conversational follow-up prompts rather than traditional line-by-line debugging.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** In what specific business scenarios is rapid, flawed prototyping more valuable than perfectly structured, maintainable code?

## Slide 8: The Democratization of Software Creation
- Vibe coding allows non-technical workers (in marketing, legal, sales) to build functional digital products.
- Amateurs can spin up Minimum Viable Products (MVPs), internal tools, and workflow automations over a weekend without knowing a programming language.
- This represents a massive shift of software development power from the IT department to the rest of the organization.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** If anyone in any department can create software, how does the role of the traditional enterprise IT department need to adapt?

## Slide 9: Vibe Coding vs. Professional Engineering
- As the trend matured, a strict line emerged between "vibe coding" and "AI-assisted engineering."
- Vibe coding is highly unstructured and exploratory, best suited for hobbyists, learning, and throwaway projects.
- AI-assisted engineering requires structured systems where developers understand software architecture, constraints, and trade-offs.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** How can a company safely encourage the creative experimentation of vibe coding without risking the stability of their production environments?

## Slide 10: Spec-Driven Development
- In professional environments, AI is used alongside rigorous technical specifications.
- Developers use AI to generate code, write unit tests, and perform security reviews based on deep architectural planning.
- The human engineer retains full ownership, comprehension, and accountability of the codebase.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** What are the essential components of a "rigorous technical spec" when you are delegating tasks to an AI rather than a human developer?

## Slide 11: Accelerated Technical Debt
- A major operational risk in 2026 is that AI tools can write flawed or poorly integrated code at lightning speed.
- Applications built purely on vibes can become incredibly brittle over time.
- Writing bad code faster leads to massive accumulations of technical debt that teams must eventually reconcile.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** How should engineering teams measure and manage technical debt when code is being generated at ten times the traditional speed?

## Slide 12: The "Maintenance Cliff"
- Vibe coders often do not understand the underlying logic of the software they create.
- The "maintenance cliff" occurs when an app becomes too complex for the AI to fix via a simple prompt, but the human creator lacks the skill to fix it manually.
- Hitting this cliff often results in the project being abandoned or requiring a total rewrite from scratch.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** Have you ever built or relied on a system you didn't fully understand? How did you handle the situation when it eventually broke?

## Slide 13: Security Vulnerabilities and AI
- Blindly accepting AI-generated code introduces severe security risks into enterprise software.
- Professional teams are mitigating this by pivoting agentic AI to act as automated, adversarial security reviewers.
- AI is now routinely forced to analyze its own outputs for vulnerabilities before any code is approved for deployment.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** Should we trust AI to audit its own code for security flaws, or does security review fundamentally require a human perspective?

## Slide 14: The Illusion of Competence
- Writing reliable software is not just about typing syntax; it requires specifying edge cases, designing user experiences, and planning for scale.
- A high-level natural language prompt is often a "wish list" rather than a true engineering blueprint.
- The lowered barrier to entry has flooded the market with apps that work flawlessly in ideal conditions but break under real-world strain.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** How do we train the next generation of technologists to spot edge cases when the AI does the heavy lifting for them?

## Slide 15: The Shift: Instruction vs. Orchestration
- We are experiencing a historical shift in human-computer interaction, comparable to the invention of the graphical user interface.
- We have moved from a paradigm of *instruction* (telling computers exactly *how* to do something) to *orchestration* (telling computers *what* we want to achieve).
- The focus of human labor is shifting from syntax generation to systems thinking and problem-solving.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** As we fully transition to an orchestration mindset, what traditional tech skills will become obsolete, and what new skills will become the most valuable?

## Slide 16: Building a Maintainable Future
- For hobbyists and non-technical teams, vibe coding remains a superpower that turns ideas into reality in hours.
- For enterprise engineering teams, the goal is harnessing Agentic systems responsibly.
- The future relies on combining the creative freedom of "vibes" with the strict guardrails of traditional software engineering.
- **Discussion question (group discussion preferred):** What is one practical step or policy your team can implement tomorrow to begin adapting to the Agentic AI and vibe coding era?
